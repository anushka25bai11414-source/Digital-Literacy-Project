## PART B- STAY SAFE ONLINE- PREVENTION CHECKLIST FOR COLLEGE STUDENTS
-----------------------------------------------------------------------

As College students, we use UPI almost every day for food, shopping, fees, and transfers,so, a little awareness can prevent major loss.
These are some precautions we can take to combat Cyber risks:
-----------------------------------------------------------------------

1. No matter how genuine they sound, don't ever give your OTP or UPI PIN to anyone. Banks never ask for it.
2. Before you accept a "Collect Request," take a moment to look at who sent it. Say no if you're not sure.
3. Before sending money, always double-check the UPI ID or name. A single wrong digit could send the money to the wrong person.
4. Don't click on payment links that come to you through WhatsApp, Instagram, or SMS.
5. Use a strong password, your fingerprint, or Face ID to secure access to your phone.
6. Don't pay for things over public Wi-Fi, like in cafés or train stations, as there are possible risks of Pin leaks.
7. To stay safe from new security threats, you should regularly update your UPI apps.
8. If you suspect someone is trying to trick you into scams, go to the National Cyber Crime Portal right away: https://cybercrime.gov.in. and report them.
9. If you think you've been a victim of financial fraud, call the Cyber Crime Helpline at 1930 right away. If you act quickly, you have a better chance of getting your money back.
